<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding the Apotheosis System">

The Apotheosis system is the heart of Lucid Blocks. It rejects the idea of a standard crafting menu, instead forcing players to interact with a fusion interface. Every item in the game possesses an internal "essence" or property. When you place items into the six available slots, the game calculates the combined essence to determine the output.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Essence Logic">
    Items with similar properties produce predictable tools. Mixing unrelated items creates bizarre artifacts or useless junk.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dilution Mechanic">
    Adding low-tier blocks (dirt/sand) to a recipe can increase the output quantity without changing the item type.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Complexity Scaling">
    Fusing two items yields simple results, while using all six slots increases the chance of rare mutations.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip: The Death Transmutation">
In Lucid Blocks, dying is not just a setback. Upon death, your entire inventory is automatically "apotheosized" together. You will respawn far away with a collection of random, fused items. This encourages a nomadic playstyle where building a permanent base is secondary to constant exploration.
</Callout>

The system essentially treats matter as a liquid state. If you put a pickaxe back into the fusion grid with another item, it may revert into sticks or transmute into a different tool entirely. This "matter transfer" is the fundamental law of the world.

<Section icon={Map} title="Mobility Gear and Artifacts">

Because the world generation in Lucid Blocks is extremely vertical—often featuring massive pits and floating islands—mobility gear is not optional. You will need a way to descend safely from the surface "Divide" to the biomes below.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Craft a Feather">
    Combine **Wool, a Stick, and a Sand Block**. This is the base component for almost all flight-related gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble the Glider Glove">
    Fuse the **Feather with more Wool**. This allows you to slide through the air, negating fall damage and allowing for long-distance travel.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Secure a Grappling Hook">
    Combine **Mana Gel and String** (or Mercury Tendril and Wool). This is essential for climbing the vertical skyscrapers in the Broken City biomes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade to Golden Boots">
    Fuse a **Leaky Pendant and Mercury Tendril**. These boots drastically alter weight physics and provide a health boost, making platforming much safer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Exploration Tip">
If you find a **Tall Brick Tower**, head inside immediately. The pots inside often drop Mana Gel and String, allowing you to craft a Grappling Hook much earlier than usual.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Story Progression and the Rosary">

While Lucid Blocks is a "feelings-first" sandbox, there is a vague narrative involving entities like the Deva (or The Lord). Progression is tracked through a **Rosary** found in your inventory.

To progress the story, you must locate **Glass Boxes** hidden within structures like the Tall Brick Towers or Gold Sanctuaries. Interacting with these boxes transports you to a surreal cutscene.

1.  **The Leaky Pendant**: Use this item to follow particles that point toward the nearest Glass Box.
2.  **The Tall Brick Tower**: Contains a Glass Box where an entity questions your divinity and warns of "The Lord's" return.
3.  **The Gold Sanctuary**: A massive sky structure where an entity claims to remember your name (pulling from your actual computer username).
4.  **Lighting the Beads**: Each successful interaction lights a bead on your Rosary. Completing the Rosary is the ultimate goal of the current 2026 build.

<Callout type="tip" title="Invisible XP">
Attacking enemies and completing story cutscenes grants "Invisible XP." There are no level-up bars, but you will occasionally be prompted to increase stats like **Mush, Leap, Fluid, or Faith**.
</Callout>
