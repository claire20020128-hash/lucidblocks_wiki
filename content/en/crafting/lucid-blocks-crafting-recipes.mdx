<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Apotheosis System: Mechanics of Essence">

The heart of progression lies in the Apotheosis System. Rather than following a fixed menu, you interact with a six-slot grid. The game analyzes the "essence" of every item placed within the grid to determine the output. This means that similar materials often yield predictable results, while radical combinations can produce "mutations" or unique artifacts. Understanding how to manipulate these slots is the difference between wasting rare resources and building a legendary kit.

One of the most critical hidden mechanics is **Dilution**. For basic items, you do not need to fill all six slots with high-value materials. By using one or two core ingredients and filling the remaining slots with "filler" like Dirt or Sand, you can significantly increase the number of items produced. However, be warned: attempting this with high-tier artifacts or specialized tools will often cause the fusion to fail or result in a "Useless Junk" item.

<Callout type="warning" title="Dilution Risks">
Never use filler blocks when crafting high-tier weapons like the Azrael or specialized artifacts. Dilution only works for basic blocks, consumables, and low-tier components. Over-diluting a complex recipe will destroy your primary materials.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Hammer} title="Essential Tool Recipes for Early Progression">

In the early hours of Lucid Blocks, your primary goal is resource acquisition. Standard tools like the Wooden Pick are easy to craft but lack durability and speed. To progress into the deeper layers of the world, you must upgrade to metal-based or elemental tools. The **lucid blocks crafting recipes** listed below are the most efficient choices for clearing the early-game hurdles.

The **Frost Pick** (or Ice Pick) is widely considered the best early-game investment. It offers significantly higher mining speed than the Wooden or Bone variants and is relatively cheap if you can locate Osmium deposits. For those focusing on gathering organic materials, the **Leaf Pick** is a specialized tool that, while fragile, allows for the instant harvesting of sticks and berries from foliage.

| Tool Name | Ingredients | Primary Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Wooden Pick** | Stick + Dirt / Log / Sand | Basic mining and block breaking |
| **Leaf Pick** | Stick + Wooden Pick | Instant harvesting of leaves and berries |
| **Bone Pick** | Bone + String | Mid-tier durability for stone mining |
| **Frost Pick** | Stick + Osmium / Flionide | High-speed mining for early ores |
| **Plum Axe** | Wooden Pick + Magnetite | Advanced woodcutting and heavy clearing |
| **Pickaxe** | Wooden Pick + Aluminum (x3) | Standard heavy-duty mining tool |

<Callout type="tip" title="Sustainability Tip">
Always keep a stack of Sticks and String in your inventory. Most tools can be crafted on the fly without a dedicated workbench, allowing you to stay in the field longer without returning to your base.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Mobility Gear and Survival Artifacts">

Exploration is the core of the experience, but the terrain is often vertical and treacherous. Crafting mobility gear is not optional—it is a requirement for reaching lay lines and high-altitude structures. The **Bee Glove** (also known as the Glider Glove) is the most important utility item you can craft early on, as it allows you to slide through the air and negate fall damage.

When paired with the **Metal Hook** or a **Grappler**, the Bee Glove allows for "slingshot" movement. By launching yourself with the hook and then deploying the glove, you can cover vast distances that would otherwise be impossible to traverse.

| **Squirrel Ring** | Bug Ring + Cut Wood | Passive +1 Jump Height |
